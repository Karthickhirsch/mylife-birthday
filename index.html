How the weeks actually went

  • Week 1

    Starting with “nothing good enough out of the box”

    I didn’t want a store-bought card. I sketched the idea, tore it up, started over, and asked myself: What would make her feel seen? The answer was: something only we share — panda heart, your name in the right places, little secrets between us.

  • Weeks 2–3

    Late nights, rewrites, and “one more” animation

    Most of this is plain HTML, CSS, and JavaScript — hand-tuned, not a drag-and-drop app. I chased bugs, slowed down things that felt rushed, sped up things that felt dead, and tested the proposal flow and games way more than you’ll ever need to know. Coffee helped. You were the point.

  • Last stretch

    Details you might not notice (I still fussed over them)

    How the confetti hits on “yes,” how the music fades, how a button reads on a phone, whether a line sounds sweet or try-hard. If something felt off, I redid it. Not because the internet cares — because I care how it feels to you.

  • For you, today

    All of it, one reason

    Every line, every color, every silly mini-game: so you’d have a birthday that feels designed for you — and so you’d never have to wonder if you were worth the effort. You are. This is just my proof in pixels.

Built with (in plain terms)

Not magic — stacked time on top of normal tools. If you care about the labels:

Hand-written HTML & CSS JavaScript (interactions, games, form) Animations & motion Formspree (so messages reach me) A lot of patience in DevTools

A real note

I’m not a big studio. I’m someone who sat down, searched errors, and learned pieces of this because it was the best way I could show up for you. There may be a typo somewhere or a quirk on an older phone — I’ve done my best. What I hope you feel isn’t “perfect code,” it’s that I chose to build something, not buy something — and that the rough edges are part of it being real. Thank you for being worth that time.
